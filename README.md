# SMS Privacy Statement

**Harrison T. Barrow III, Attorney at Law, PC**
Last updated: [DATE]

This SMS Privacy Statement describes how Harrison T. Barrow III, Attorney at Law, PC (the "Firm") collects, uses, discloses, and safeguards information when you communicate with the Firm by SMS text message ("Program"). This statement supplements, and does not replace, the Firm's general privacy practices and its obligations under the Massachusetts Rules of Professional Conduct.

## 1. Information We Collect

Through the Program, we may collect:

- **Contact information**, including your mobile telephone number, name, and, if you provide them, your email address and mailing address;
- **Message content**, including the text of messages you send to the Firm and the Firm sends to you, together with date and time stamps;
- **Metadata** generated by carriers and our SMS service provider, including delivery status, message identifiers, and routing information;
- **Consent records**, including the date, time, source, and IP address (when applicable) of your opt-in; and
- **Opt-out records**, including the date and time of any STOP request.

We do not collect mobile device geolocation, contact lists, photographs, or other device data through the Program.

## 2. How We Use This Information

We use the information collected through the Program to:

- Communicate with you about your legal matter or inquiry;
- Schedule, confirm, and remind you of appointments and court dates;
- Respond to your questions and requests;
- Maintain records of attorney-client communications consistent with the Firm's file-retention policy;
- Comply with carrier rules, applicable law, and the Massachusetts Rules of Professional Conduct; and
- Investigate and respond to suspected abuse, fraud, or misuse of the Program.

We do **not** use Program information for advertising, marketing, or promotional purposes. We do **not** sell your information.

## 3. Disclosure of Information

We disclose information collected through the Program only as follows:

- **To our SMS service provider** and its underlying carriers and aggregators, as necessary to transmit and deliver messages. These providers are contractually obligated to handle data only as needed to deliver the service. **Mobile information will not be shared with third parties or affiliates for marketing or promotional purposes.**
- **To Firm personnel**, including attorneys, paralegals, and staff with a need to access the information in connection with your matter.
- **In the course of representation**, where disclosure is authorized by you, required to carry out the representation, or otherwise permitted by Mass. R. Prof. C. 1.6.
- **As required by law**, including in response to a lawful subpoena, court order, or other legal process; in connection with a regulatory inquiry; or to comply with a reporting obligation. The Firm will assert the attorney-client privilege and work-product protection where applicable.
- **To protect rights and safety**, where disclosure is necessary to prevent imminent harm or to protect the Firm's legal rights.

## 4. Data Security

The Firm maintains a Written Information Security Program (WISP) and administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect personal information, consistent with 201 CMR 17.00 (*Standards for the Protection of Personal Information of Residents of the Commonwealth*). Safeguards include access controls, encryption of data at rest where supported by our systems, secure backups, and personnel training.

You should be aware that SMS messages are transmitted over carrier networks that are not end-to-end encrypted. No system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee the security of information transmitted by SMS. See the related discussion in our [SMS Terms and Conditions]([T&C URL]), §8.

## 5. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a breach of security affecting personal information of Massachusetts residents, the Firm will provide notice in accordance with G.L. c. 93H, § 3 and any applicable federal law or rule.

## 6. Retention

The Firm retains SMS records as part of the client file for the period required by its file-retention policy and applicable Bar guidance. Records related to closed matters are retained consistent with that policy and then destroyed in a manner designed to protect their confidentiality. Records related to non-clients and prospective clients who do not retain the Firm are retained for a shorter period sufficient to document the inquiry and disposition.

## 7. Your Choices and Rights

- **Opt out at any time** by replying STOP to any message. See the [SMS Terms and Conditions]([T&C URL]) for details.
- **Access and correction**: existing clients may request access to communications in their file consistent with applicable law and the Firm's file-access policy. Other persons may contact the Firm to inquire about information held about them.
- **Closing the file**: at the conclusion of representation, you may make arrangements with the Firm for the disposition of your file, including any SMS records contained in it.

Massachusetts does not currently provide a general consumer right to deletion of records held by a law firm in connection with the representation of a client; certain records must be retained to comply with professional and legal obligations.

## 8. Children

The Program is not directed to children under 18, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18 through the Program.
